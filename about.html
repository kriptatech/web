About
 
 
 
Director Mahesh Rao has a pleasant surprise for you in the form of narration. It is a romantic movie
with unique flow of the sequences. With excellent script, Mahesh Rao takes you through the journey of the travails and trauma of a
hero who has been ditched by his lover to marry another man. Director calls the incident as heroine preferring 'green card' (rich)
for ration card (poor). Mahesh Rao impresses with the unique way of narration. It is serious and sometimes you may feel the story is
not moving at all, but the director needs a pat for presenting the story in a 'non- Gandhinagar' style.

Murali (Prajwal) falls in love with Shwetha (Harshika Poonacha). When everything seem to be okay, Shwetha gives a shock to Murali by
marrying another person without informing him. The dejected Murali meets Meera (Reema Vohra) near a traffic signal.
The talkative Meera becomes guide and philosopher for Murali when he narrates his love story to her. He becomes emotionally

attached to her to such an extent that he decides to marry her. Meera too has a story of falling in love with Bharath who cheats her
and forces her to become a sex worker. After hearing her story, will Murali marry her?
Full marks to Prajwal Devaraj for his brilliant performance. Reema Vohra is simply superb.
Harshika shines. Music by Abhiman Roy and camera by Jai Anand are impressive.
